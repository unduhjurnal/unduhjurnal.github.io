19. Jelaskan dengan lengkap tentang Main Idea dan Supporting Details beserta contohnya.


Grasping the Main Idea:
A paragraph is a group of sentences related to a particular topic, or central theme. Every paragraph has a key concept or main idea. The main idea is the most important piece of information the author wants you to know about the concept of that paragraph.
When authors write they have an idea in mind that they are trying to get across. This is especially true as authors compose paragraphs. An author organizes each paragraph's main idea and supporting details in support of the topic or central theme, and each paragraph supports the paragraph preceding it.
A writer will state his/her main idea explicitly somewhere in the paragraph. That main idea may be stated at the beginning of the paragraph, in the middle, or at the end. The sentence in which the main idea is stated is the topic sentence of that paragraph.
The topic sentence announces the general theme ( or portion of the theme) to be dealt with in the paragraph. Although the topic sentence may appear anywhere in the paragraph, it is usually first - and for a very good reason. This sentence provides the focus for the writer while writing and for the reader while reading. When you find the topic sentence, be sure to underline it so that it will stand out not only now, but also later when you review.

36. Apa yang dimaksud main idea dari suatu teks dan berikan contohnya?


Yang dimaksud main idea dari suatu teks yakni sebuah gagasan yang menjadi dasar terbentuknya sebuah paragraf. Dalam sebuah teks main idea dapat diletakan di awal, akhir, awal dan akhir, atau di seluruh paragraf.

Pembahasan

Dalam teks berbahasa Inggris, main idea atau ide pokok merupakan sebuah gagasan yang menjadi dasar terbentuknya sebuah paragraf.

Main idea berasal dari sebuah masalah yang akan dicari jalan keluarnya melalui paragraf dengan didukung oleh beberapa kalimat penjelas berupa fakta maupun opini.

Contoh main idea dalam teks Malin Kundang

Once upon a time, there was a poor woman who had lost her husband and had a son named Malin Kundang. One day, Malin Kundang went sailing to another place. Since Malin Kundang left, his mother went to the shore every day, waiting for Malin Kundang to return.

After years, her waiting ended when a luxurious ship docked at the shore. A young couple in extravagant clothes stepped down from the ship. Malin's mother was sure that the young man was her son. She tried to embrace him, but he threw her away. He didn't admit the woman as his mother.

Being denied and humiliated, Malin's mother cursed her son. After a moment, the luxurious ship was attacked by a hurricane, and the cursed son turned into a rock.

Main idea dari paragrafi 1 adalah Malin Kundang went sailing to another place.Main idea dari paragrafi 2 adalah Malin Kundang didn't admit the woman as his mother.Main idea dari paragrafi 3 adalah Malin's mother cursed her son into a rock.

40. buatlah 3 make a dialogue:-giving a suggestion or on idea-giving agreement with a suggestion or on idea-giving disagreement with a suggestion or on idea gambar tersebut hanya sebagai contoh tugas besok di kumpul​


Dialogue: Giving a Suggestion, Agreement, and Disagreement

Alex: I was thinking, instead of going out for dinner again, we could try cooking a new recipe together at home.

Emily: That's a great suggestion, Alex! It could be a fun and cost-effective way to spend the evening.

Jake: How about we organize a beach cleanup event this weekend?

Linda: I completely agree, Jake. It's important to contribute to our community and protect the environment.

Sophia: I think we should invest all our savings in cryptocurrency.

Mark: I have to disagree, Sophia. Cryptocurrency can be very volatile, and it's risky to put all our savings into it. We should diversify our investments instead.

Person A: "I think our company should start an employee wellness program."

Person B: "That's a great idea. It could improve employee morale and productivity."

Person C: "I'm not so sure about that. We have a tight budget, and I'm not convinced it would be cost-effective."

In this dialogue, Person A gives a suggestion or idea, Person B agrees with it, and Person C expresses disagreement by mentioning budget constraints.
