2. bagaimana cara mengerjakan bahasa Inggris judulnya reading atau membaca​


Jawaban:

Berikut adalah langkah-langkah untuk mengerjakan reading atau membaca dalam bahasa Inggris:

1. Baca judul atau topik dengan teliti - pastikan bahwa Anda mengerti apa yang akan dibahas dalam teks tersebut.

2. Baca teks secara keseluruhan - ini akan membantu Anda memahami gambaran besar tentang apa yang akan dibahas dalam teks.

3. Baca teks lagi dengan lebih teliti - kali ini, cari informasi lebih detail mengenai topik yang dibahas dalam teks.

4. Buat catatan - setiap kali menemukan informasi penting, buatlah catatan atau coret-coret di samping teks tersebut agar lebih mudah untuk dikembalikan nanti.

5. Cari pemahaman dari konteks - jika terdapat kata atau frasa yang tidak Anda mengerti, coba cari tahu artinya dengan melihat konteks kalimat atau mencari definisi di kamus.

6. Cari informasi terkait - setelah selesai membaca, jangan lupa untuk mencari informasi tambahan mengenai topik yang dibahas dalam teks tersebut.

7. Latih kecepatan membaca - teruslah berlatih untuk meningkatkan kecepatan membaca Anda sehingga Anda dapat membaca teks dalam waktu yang lebih singkat tanpa kehilangan pemahaman terhadap isi teks.

Dengan mengikuti langkah-langkah tersebut, Anda akan dapat mengerjakan reading atau membaca dalam bahasa Inggris dengan lebih efektif dan efisien.

9. buatlah thesis dalam bahasa Inggris tentang project ocean cleanup dengan 5 argumentasi tolong ya butuh bgt​


Berikut adalah contoh thesis dalam bahasa Inggris tentang proyek Ocean Cleanup dengan 5 argumen pendukung:

Thesis Statement:

"The Ocean Cleanup project represents a critical and innovative initiative to address the growing issue of plastic pollution in our oceans. This thesis explores its significance by examining five key arguments related to its environmental impact, technological advancements, cost-effectiveness, global collaboration, and potential for long-term change."

Argument 1: Environmental Impact

One of the primary arguments supporting the Ocean Cleanup project is its substantial positive environmental impact. By deploying advanced technology to remove large quantities of plastic waste from the ocean, this project contributes significantly to mitigating the harm caused to marine ecosystems, wildlife, and ocean health.

Argument 2: Technological Advancements

The project showcases impressive technological advancements in the field of environmental cleanup. Innovative systems such as the "Interceptor" and the "System 002" demonstrate the feasibility of scalable solutions for tackling plastic pollution in large water bodies, offering valuable insights for future initiatives and industries.

Argument 3: Cost-Effectiveness

The Ocean Cleanup project demonstrates its cost-effectiveness compared to other strategies for plastic pollution mitigation. By leveraging natural ocean currents to transport plastic waste to collection points, the project minimizes operational costs, making it an efficient approach for addressing a global crisis.

Argument 4: Global Collaboration

The project fosters international collaboration and cooperation in addressing a shared environmental challenge. Its global reach, partnerships with governments, NGOs, and businesses, and collaborative efforts to reduce plastic pollution make it an emblematic example of the importance of working together on a global scale.

Argument 5: Potential for Long-Term Change

Finally, the Ocean Cleanup project carries the potential for long-term change by raising awareness about the urgent need to reduce plastic consumption and promote recycling. Its success can inspire governments, industries, and individuals worldwide to adopt more sustainable practices, ultimately preventing plastic waste from entering our oceans.

This thesis aims to underscore the significance of the Ocean Cleanup project in addressing plastic pollution while emphasizing the need for continued research, support, and global action to protect our oceans for future generations.

SINGKAT:

Thesis Statement:

"The Ocean Cleanup project is a vital initiative with significant environmental impact, innovative technology, cost-effectiveness, global collaboration, and potential for long-term change."

1. Environmental Impact: Reduces plastic harm to oceans.

2. Technological Advancements: Showcases scalable cleanup tech.

3. Cost-Effective: Efficient use of natural ocean currents.

4. Global Collaboration: Fosters international cooperation.

5. Long-Term Change: Inspires sustainable practices worldwide.

Here's a reiteration and conclusion:

Reiteration:

"In summary, the Ocean Cleanup project offers an innovative and cost-effective solution to combat plastic pollution, encouraging global collaboration and raising awareness."

Conclusion:

"In conclusion, the Ocean Cleanup project holds great promise in addressing plastic pollution. However, it also highlights the need for broader efforts to reduce plastic consumption and improve waste management globally. Together, we can safeguard our oceans for the future."

13. soal reading bahasa inggris beserta jawabannya


1.Stalagmites are formed by …

(A) drops of water which enter through cracks in the ceiling.

(B) underground rivers which flow through the cave.

(C) water dripping from an overhead stalactite.

(D) water which trickles down a slope.

Jawaban : (C) water dripping from an overhead stalactite.

2. Sinkholes are …

(A) the decorative dripstone features found in caves.

(B) natural openings on the surface that lead to caves.

(C) colorful layers of mineral deposits.

(D) None of the above

Jawaban : (B) natural openings on the surface that lead to caves.

3. Which speleothem grows upward from the floor?

(A) Stalagmites

(B) Stalactites

(C) Sinkholes

(D) Curtains

Jawaban : (A) Stalagmites

4. An “inclined ceiling” is one which …

(A) is straight.

(B) is crooked.

(C) is slanted.

(D) is wet.

Jawaban : (C) is slanted.

5. Which of the following are NOT caused by dripping water?

(A) Stalactites

(B) Stalagmites

(C) Slopes

(D) Curtains

Jawaban : (C) Slopes

6. The information in the passage is most relevant to which field of study?

(A) Geography

(B) Archaeology

(C) Physics

(D) Geology

Jawaban : (D) Geology

7. “ Curtains” can also be called …

(A) column.

(B) draperies.

(C) stalagmites.

(D) rims.

Jawaban : (B) draperies.

8. The word speleothem comes from which language?

(A) Latin

(B) French

(C) Greek

(D) English

Jawaban : (C) Greek

9. Stalagmites are formed by …

(A) drops of water which enter the cave through cracks in the ceiling.

(B) underground rivers which flow through the cave.

(C) water which seeps through the cave floor.

(D) water which trickles down a slope.

Jawaban : (A) drops of water which enter the cave through cracks in the ceiling.

10. Which speleothem hangs from the ceiling of a cave?

(A) Stalagmites

(B) Stalactites

(C) Columns

(D) Rimstones

Jawaban : (B) Stalactites

Jawaban:

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by a newly discovered coronavirus.

Most people infected with the COVID-19 virus will experience mild to moderate respiratory illness and recover without requiring special treatment. Older people, and those with underlying medical problems like cardiovascular disease, diabetes, chronic respiratory disease, and cancer are more likely to develop serious illness.

The best way to prevent and slow down transmission is be well informed about the COVID-19 virus, the disease it causes and how it spreads. Protect yourself and others from infection by washing your hands or using an alcohol based rub frequently and not touching your face.

The COVID-19 virus spreads primarily through droplets of saliva or discharge from the nose when an infected person coughs or sneezes, so it’s important that you also practice respiratory etiquette (for example, by coughing into a flexed elbow).

At this time, there are no specific vaccines or treatments for COVID-19. However, there are many ongoing clinical trials evaluating potential treatments. WHO will continue to provide updated information as soon as clinical findings become available.
